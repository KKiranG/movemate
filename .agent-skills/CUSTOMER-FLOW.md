# Customer Flow

## Core promise

Customers should understand that they are booking into real spare capacity, not requesting a quote.

The product should make three things obvious:
- this trip already exists
- this is why the price is lower
- this is what happens next

## Current customer journey

### Search

Search is public.

Inputs focus on:
- from
- to
- when
- what

Output should emphasize:
- route fit
- timing
- available space
- transparent pricing
- carrier trust signals

### No-result path

Saved searches convert dead-end demand into a re-engagement path.
Do not treat the empty state as an afterthought.

### Booking

Booking requires auth and should feel finite:

1. open trip detail
2. add item details
3. add pickup and dropoff details
4. review transparent pricing
5. authorize payment
6. receive confirmation

### Post-booking

Customers can:
- track booking status
- confirm receipt
- open disputes
- leave a review

## Anti-patterns

- turning search into a quote-request funnel
- hiding fees until the end
- making booking feel like open-ended negotiation
